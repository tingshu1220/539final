SURVAY

65% of respondents express dissatisfaction with traditional method to allocation household responsibilities. 

I received 52 surveys responses, investigating the pain points of chores allocation among users. 67% of individuals express that they prefer coordinating tasks verbally. 32% opt to handle their own chores.

So, why traditional method and other Apps failed?

USER INTERVIEWS

Users' needs vary significantly based on income levels and roles within the household.

conducted a semi-structured interviews with 8 families representing diverse ageand individuals in various living situations  to explore the challenges.

Baby Boomer

For single-income households,  it's hard to change their household chore patterns. 

Millennials

Most Millennials are focused on issues related to childcare responsibilities.

​Gen-Z

Gen Z expresses a desire for their roommates or partners to have better lifestyle habits.

Challenge:
1. Disparities in income structures result in the inability to achieve household chore equality.
2. Fragmented tasks are not likely to be recorded through digital device.
New Opportunity:
Involving children can alleviate the burden on parents by making them a part of the chore distribution.

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

Chore apps regularly end up forcing women to take on the additional burden of using technology to assign tasks.

UNDERSTANDING USERS

I defined our personas based on two categories based on their relationship and communication styles: Initial Connections v.s Intimate Relationships
Initial Connections

Initial connections can be roommates who are not well-acquainted with each other and seek personal space.

Intimate Relationships

Intimate relationships can be couples or family members, who are deeply connected and in search of improved communication methods.

DESIGN GOAL

Transforming the completion of tasks into a rewarding and bonding experience that enhances interpersonal relationships.
1. Transparency

Efficient task allocation methods with responsibility tracking.

2. Incentivization

After completing a task, gain motivation and feedback.

Encourage all family members to use the app including various age groups and genders.

3. Accessibility

2. Iteration towards Incentivization:

Tag Interface and Interaction Exploration

At first, I design a traditional to do list. However, I want the experience to be more engaging to the user and gamify the process.

Swipe right to accomplish tasks and prevent errors.

Unveil your rewards with a satisfying gesture. Experience streamlined task completion, enhancing satisfaction, and receive instant gratification through a rewarding pop-up showcasing earned points. Enjoy the flexibility of an undo button to retract actions if needed.

3.Iteration towards Accessibility

How do we ensure the app's inclusivity across various age groups and genders involves addressing the diverse needs and preferences of users?

​Color theme for all gender

Given that 74% of housework is typically managed by women, I chose the color green as a gender-neutral symbol to encourage all family members to use the app including various gender and age groups.

Design for elder and visional impairment

 I conducted Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) testing to ensure that the chosen color is effective in various situations.

Balancing business objective and resouces
Cut down the edge feature for parenting and focus on the product for all

Initially,  the target audience encompassed parents and childcare, where I designed feature for parenting and product for children. However,  considering 80% of the product users won't use the feature and there are alternative App for parent-child communication , I decided to remove the education-related components and create features for all.

User Testing

I created a click-through prototype using Figma for conducting usability tests to gain deep insights into user interactions with the new interface. Over two weeks, I conducted 40 tests with both tutors and learners from our existing user base. I developed a structured user testing plan, designed tasks in the prototype, observed users via screen sharing on Zoom, and gathered feedback. The feedback included positive reactions and areas for improvement, which I integrated into the final design. Engaging with users and observing their reactions motivated me to make thoughtful design decisions that benefit our users.
